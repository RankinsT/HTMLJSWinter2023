Review

Tyler Rankins 3/6/2023

I loved Pacific Rim for its action and its intention to be realistic in the department of giant, towering, pilotable mechs. The power that the Jeagers were shown to have
alongside their realistic slow movement enraptured me while watching the movie. In addition to its action and as much realism you could have with giant robots and giant monsters, I loved
Pacific Rim's sound design presented for the towering mechs and Collosal Kaiju's throughout the movie. I'd say my favorite Jeager from the movie would either be
Cherno Alpha, because of its simple yet devastating design. Or I would also choose Crimson Typhoon for its creative and well visualized "Thundercloud" formation with three pilots instead of the normal two.

Trivia

  • According to one of the actors in the movie, the sets made while filming were very unique and extremely detailed.
    So much so that if you looked into a car on set, it would have a receipt from a store that exists in the movie.
  • The sounds from the Kaiju were made with animal roars and growls with a touch of human samples that were filtered, sped up, and slowed down.
  • del Toro, the director, stated that over 200 Jeagers and Kaiju were designed while only a dozen or so made an appearance in the movie.
